About me

"Sometimes the one thing that changes everything is just a single word."

I was eager to try new things from an early age. I had many hobbies: various sports, visual arts, music — I wrote poetry and recorded my own radio programmes in English on a cassette recorder. I was interested in a bit of everything. But what fascinated me most was why we humans behave the way we do.

My interest in people led me for many years to early childhood education with children with special needs and their families. I discovered how small things can have a truly great impact. When you change one thing, everything might change. That one thing can be as small as a single word. I loved my work with children and families.

A developer's mindset later took me to other roles: if something is not working, I start looking at what I could do differently — and then I do it.

The desire to keep learning also led me, for a time, into studying sales and marketing — and eventually into working as a salesperson, and later as a stand-in store manager at a jewellery shop. That detour taught me plenty: how to meet customers, the dynamics of sales, and how to deliver the store's best result during the busiest summer season.

Child welfare work and management responsibilities took me into the most difficult moments of people's lives. I had the privilege of walking alongside families — listening, finding solutions, keeping hope alive even when it felt distant for everyone involved. Multiprofessional collaboration, high-pressure situations, and the ability to stay calm and clear-headed in demanding moments became strengths I could not have developed any other way. Even the smallest step forward in a client's everyday life could mean an entirely different direction for their life. This was my heart's work.

Trying new things and the joy of learning still keeps me moving. I always find something new to explore — whether that's university studies, colourwork knitting, or artificial intelligence.

In recent years I have been able to combine marketing and communications with everything I have always cared about: understanding people, creativity, writing, continuous learning, and technology. Do get in touch if you think my skills could be of use to you.

In my free time I prefer to move in nature with my dog Alma or go cross-country skiing. I unwind by singing, listening to audiobooks, and exploring topics from all walks of life. Family is the unquestionable number one on my list of what matters most.
