About me

"My brother became a computer technician.
I kept studying people."

I have always wanted to understand why people behave the way they do. As a child I played sports, made art, and played music — I also wrote poetry and recorded my own radio programmes in English on a cassette recorder. I was curious about everything.

My mother bought us a Commodore 128 when everyone else had a 64. Together we typed a screensaver from the manual and got it running on the living-room TV.

That curiosity about people led me to work with children with special needs and their families. A developer's mindset took me further: when something isn't working, I start looking for what could be done differently — and then I do it. What works well doesn't need to be changed; it needs to be strengthened.

The joy of learning keeps me moving. I always find something new to explore — whether that's university studies, colourwork knitting, or artificial intelligence.

In recent years I have been lucky enough to combine everything I have always cared about: understanding people, creativity, writing, continuous learning, and technology. An unusual combination, perhaps — but it is exactly what makes me who I am.

Family is the unquestionable number one on my list of what matters most.
